Key Takeaways

  • The video introduces a practical progression from single-agent systems to sequential and parallel multi-agent workflows.
  • Single agents are easy to build and flexible, but they become less reliable when prompts have to encode too much logic.
  • Sequential agents trade flexibility for control by forcing a fixed order and passing results through shared session state.
  • Parallel agents reduce latency for independent subtasks, but they usually need an aggregation step and can increase cost.
  • The next episode will expand into more advanced coordination patterns like critique loops, coordinator routing, and agents used as tools.
